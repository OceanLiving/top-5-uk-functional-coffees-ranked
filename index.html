What's Wrong With Most Mushroom Coffees?

Mushroom coffee is marketed as a smarter, healthier way to start your day. But when we looked closely at the most popular brands in the UK, we found a different story. Many products rely on instant coffee powders, use fillers like maltodextrin, and contain tiny amounts of actual mushrooms.

These compromises mean you may be paying premium prices for:

  • Low mushroom content (100–300mg per serving vs 1,000mg+ needed for benefits)
  • Instant mixes that lack the richness of fresh coffee
  • Non-organic mushrooms that may carry impurities
  • Artificial flavours to mask poor quality

In short: not all mushroom coffees live up to their claims.

What to Look For in a Quality Mushroom Coffee

Not all mushroom coffees are created equal. If you want a blend that truly supports focus, energy, and wellbeing, look for these criteria:

  • Clinical-strength dosing — at least 1,000mg of mushroom extracts per serving
  • 100% fruiting bodies — no mycelium-on-grain fillers
  • Organic certification — clean, heavy-metal-free sourcing
  • Third-party lab testing — with >20% beta-glucans (the active compounds that drive real results)
  • Freshly roasted coffee — not instant powders, for true coffee flavour

These are the standards we applied when testing the UK's most popular brands.
